# Tool Progression

## Early progression
- `nmap`: Week 1 onward for basic service discovery, then repeated every phase.
- `Burp Suite`: Week 1 onward for HTTP inspection, request tampering, and web exploit validation.
- `bash` and core Linux commands: Week 1 onward for navigation, file work, shell handling, and later post-exploitation.

## Offensive Entry additions
- `gobuster` or `ffuf`: Weeks 5-8 for content discovery and web attack-surface expansion.
- `curl`: Weeks 5-8 for lightweight service and request testing.

## Intermediate additions
- `linpeas`: Week 9 onward for Linux privilege escalation confirmation after manual checks.
- `winpeas`: Week 11 onward for Windows privilege escalation confirmation after manual checks.

## OSCP-track additions
- `netcat`: Week 17 onward for lab-safe listener, shell handling, and service interaction where appropriate.
- Report templates and checklist notes: emphasized from Week 15 onward so notes stay same-day and exam-usable.

## Recurrence rules
- Every phase repeats `nmap`, `Burp Suite`, and Linux shell work.
- From Week 5 onward, content discovery recurs regularly.
- From Week 9 onward, privesc tooling recurs regularly.
- From Week 15 onward, reporting and quick-reference maintenance recur weekly.
