## Time-budget rules (non-negotiable)

Use the rules in `04_TIME_BUDGET_RULES.md` exactly.

### Summary
- Monday: 120 active minutes / 130 elapsed minutes
- Tuesday: 120 active minutes / 130 elapsed minutes
- Wednesday: 240 active minutes / 270 elapsed minutes
- Thursday: 120 active minutes / 130 elapsed minutes
- Friday: rest
- Saturday: 240 active minutes / 270 elapsed minutes
- Sunday: 240 active minutes / 270 elapsed minutes

Weekly total:
- 1080 active minutes = 18 active hours
- 1200 elapsed minutes = 20 calendar hours including pauses

### Break rule
Insert one 10-minute pause **between each completed active hour**.
That means:
- 2h active session = 2h10 elapsed
- 4h active session = 4h30 elapsed

Do not compress or overbook the schedule.

## Realism rules

The program must be realistic.

That means:
- no impossible jumps
- no 6-hour workload on a 2h day
- no 2-week gap between hands-on exercises
- no early assumption of strong Linux or web exploitation skill
- no passive-only weeks

### Weekly minimums
- Week 1–2: at least 1 hands-on practical lab each week
- Starting Week 3: at least 1 exploit attempt each week
- Starting Week 4: at least 1 machine / target writeup each week
- Every week: at least 1 learning log and 1 weekly review
- Every phase: at least 1 checkpoint note

### Consolidation
Include at least:
- one consolidation week around Week 6
- one consolidation week around Week 12
- one consolidation week around Week 18

### Late-phase realism
Include at least:
- one mock / timed OSCP-style simulation in the last 5 weeks
- one report-writing-heavy weekend in the last 4 weeks
