# Phase 3 - OSCP Track

## Purpose
Shift to PEN-200 and Proving Grounds style work, reporting, and timed simulations.

## Weeks in this phase
- Week 15: PEN-200 onboarding, Proving Grounds rhythm, and OSCP-style note discipline
- Week 16: Exam-style standalone target repetition with stronger Windows and Linux note control
- Week 17: Independent mixed-target work with stronger judgment and less hint dependence
- Week 18: Consolidation week for exam-style speed, note quality, and weak-area correction
- Week 19: Semi-timed standalone target practice with disciplined note capture
- Week 20: Report-heavy weekend and evidence packaging for OSCP-style writeups
- Week 21: Timed mini-mock with defined time boxes and same-day reporting
- Week 22: Weak-area correction after the mock with one fresh target to validate improvements
- Week 23: Full mock weekend with bounded scope, disciplined notes, and blunt debrief
- Week 24: Final consolidation, readiness summary, and post-program operating plan

## Phase milestones
- Start the phase with explicit weekly notes, deliverables, and quick-reference updates.
- End the phase with a checkpoint note and at least one report-quality artifact.

## What success looks like by the end of the phase
- PEN-200 and PG-style work feel natural inside the weekly cadence.
- Timed work, same-day reporting, and debrief habits are in place.
- A final readiness summary and next-cycle plan are grounded in artifacts.
